About

Tori Kelly is an American singer, songwriter, and actress known for her powerful vocals, soulful sound, and ability to blend pop, R&B, and gospel influences. Born Victoria Loren Kelly on December 14, 1992, in Wildomar, California, she gained widespread recognition after independently releasing her music and building a fanbase on YouTube.

Tori Kelly started her career by posting cover songs on YouTube at a young age, showcasing her impressive vocal range. At age 16, she auditioned for American Idol in 2010 but was eliminated before reaching the final rounds. Despite this setback, she continued working on her music and released an independent EP, "Handmade Songs by Tori Kelly" in 2012, which she wrote, produced, and recorded in her home studio.

Her big break came when she signed with Capitol Records and worked with legendary music manager, Scooter Braun. In 2015, Tori released her debut major-label album, "Unbreakable Smile", featuring the hit single "Nobody Love." The album received widespread acclaim, debuting at No. 2 on the Billboard 200 chart.
